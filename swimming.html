Swimming

Pros
  • There is a large growing market.
  • High percentage of high income participants.
Cons
  • Doesn't have a lot of data collection.
  • Doesn't allow advanced analytics.

Factor Analysis

Market Size and Demographics

Swimming has moderately sized participation at both the high school level and core participation of the total population ages 6-24. The sport has seen high growth in recent years in both areas.

Swimming has the highest percent of participants with a high income level out of any of the 10 sports.

Organizational Structure

Governing Body Requirements: High

There is a very strong central governing body in swimming. USA Swimming is responsible for selecting and training the Olympic team but they have member teams ranging from age 5 to adult. USA Swimming requires the scores from each meets to be reported and kept in its database. USA Swimming also keeps a record of fastest times for each event.

Team vs. Individual: Low

Each swimmer is scored individually. At swim meets, many swimmers on the same team could be competing in different events at the same time so it would be impossible for one coach to score his entire team.

Team Size: High

Swim teams are usually large because they contain many different age groups who all operate as one organization. The size varies depending on the area and the team but the largest teams have a few hundred participants.

Team Composition: Low

The same data is collected about each swimmer. There are no unique positions that have unique statistics.

Culture

Acceptance of Technology: Low

Many swim meets use electronic touch pads in the pool to record the exact time a swimmer finished a race but not many other technological advances.

Appreciation for Statistics: Low

There are not many statistics commonly used in swimming due to the simplified scoring system.

Demand for Content: Low

The only content available is the time a swimmer earned for each heat and those are easily available already.

Pain Points

Game Clock: Low

Though individual races are timed, swimming competitions are not timed as a whole so keeping track of time is not an issue.

Field Position: Low

Field position is not something that can be tracked or that is relevant in the sport of swimming.

Pace of Game: Low

There's only one data point collected at the end of each heat in a swim meet so there is no continuous game.

Benefit from Automation: Low

There is little data collection about the competition so there are not many possibilities to benefit from automation.

Additional Insights

Existing Competition: Low

There is a widely used existing system that offers swim meet management tools such as sign up, seeding, and score aggregation as well as apps for coaches to manage communication and payment and for parents that allows them to see their child's scores.

Benefits of Live Updates: Low

Fan experience would not be enriched to much from live updates because the final score is the only update they would receive.
