Track and Field

Pros
  • Huge overall participation in the sport, as it covers all three seasons with cross country, indoor track, and outdoor track
Cons
  • No room for statistical analysis, merely reporting times for races and places finished in
  • The current GameChanger model doesn’t work for a track as there are no benefits to live automation
  • Entry barrier is high due to the amount of meet organizing software that exists

Factor Analysis

Market Size and Demographics

Track has one of the highest high school participation rates, totally 1,042,348 participants according to the NHFS. It has a 5 year growth rate of 1.72% in high school. Overall the track and field community has 2,263,000 core participants, giving it a high participation rate.

The sport is pretty evenly split between the male and female genders. Males total 56.3 percent of the total population while females make up 43.7 percent.

Geographically, the sport is diverse, with no region truly dominating the sport.

Organizational Structure

Governing Body Requirements: High

USA Track & Field (USATF) oversees much of the club track scene. They have member associations but don’t require reporting to any specific database. USATF keep track of the best times and rankings by event and age group

Team vs. Individual Scoring: Low

Each event is scored individually. Depending on the place finished, each position is assigned a specific amount of points that goes towards the overall team score.

Team Size: High

Track and field programs carry a large amount of athletes due to the different events and different age groups. Organizations often have 50+ athletes in total.

Team Composition: Low

While there are different events, each event is either scored by time or distance, thus creating a low opportunity for differentiation of statistics.

Culture

Acceptance for Technology: Medium

Track is beginning to gain traction at the more advanced levels for an appreciation of new technology. Electronic laser timing is used for many events as well as GPS tracking for some cross country events.

Appreciation for Statistics: Low

There are no statistics in track beyond time and distances so there is no real way to appreciate them.

Demand for Content: Low

There is evidence of desire for play-by-play analysis at the youth level leaving a clear gap in individual player tracking.

Pain Points

Game Clock: Low

There is no overall game clock for track and field meets, but rather just individual times.

Field Position: Low

Again, this category doesn’t score for track and field as participants are required to stay in their designated lanes.

Pace of Game: Low

There is no continuous play as there is only individual times for events.

Benefit of Automation: Low

By automating scoring, the only thing you would be able to do is have live updates on placement in races, which is fairly inconsequential.

Additional Insights

Existing Competition: Low

There are many prevalent apps that have meet tracking software and keep a database of times for each meet scored using that software

Benefits from Live Updates: Low

The only way for live updates would be posting times after a race is completed which provides little ability for statistical analysis.
