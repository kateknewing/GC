Gamechanger

GameChanger has successfully entered the youth sports industry in baseball, softball, basketball, and lacrosse. After the success seen in these sports, expansion into new sports is the most logical course of action.

Using a 2% participation test described here, we were able to cut the original list of sports from 90 to 10. When analyzing these 10 sports, we chose to focus on five major categories: Market Size & Demographics, Culture, Organizational Structure, Pain Points, and Additional Insights.

Each category was broken down into different sub factors that were analyzed and scored. Each category had its own weight depending on its relevance to GameChanger’s expansion. When all the data was compromised we came out with a final listing of the 10 sports ranked according to their scoring on our system.

Youth Market


The youth sports market comprises roughly 26 million participants between the ages of 6-17. The player collectively participate in over different 90 sports at the town, school, and club level. While total youth sport participants have seen in a slight decrease in the past 5 years, those taking part have increased in intensity, thus increasing spending, growing the industry into one valued at $7 billion a year.

Initial Scoping


To narrow down the sports market of over 90 sports we began looking at high school data from the NFHS (National Federation of State High School Association). This data provided insight into the relative size of the amateur sports market from each state between the years 2002 – 2013. While GameChanger’s current clientele is much wider than high school, we believe the high school sports market to be a fair indication of the trends in youth sports.

The first on the list of priorities for GameChanger when evaluating criteria for expansion is market size. Of the over 90 sports, the overwhelming majority have such low participation rates that an expansion would be useless. In order to better understand which sports would be viable options, we began by took the sport-by-sport participation divided by the total participation. We concluding that without having 2% of the high school market share, it was an irrelevant option for GameChanger's expansion.
