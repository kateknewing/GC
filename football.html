Football

Pros
  • Largest market size of all ten sports.
  • Nearly endless opportunities for data collection and advanced analysis.
  • Culture of fan engagement and statistics lead to a high demand for content.
Cons
  • Professional leagues are slow to adapt in an analytic culture
  • Many exisitng apps and websites to track statistics.

Factor Analysis

Market Size and Demographics

Football is the sport with the highest participation at the high school level. According to NFHS, 1,094,949 high school athletes played football in the 2013-2014 school year. This accounts for 14% of total high school sportsparticipation. According to SFIA, football has the highest percent of core participants out of the other 10 sports.

Football has seen a decrease in growth - down 2.5% in total core participation and down 1.4% in high school participation.

Organizational Structure

Governing Body Requirements: Low

There are some nationwide youth programs such as Pop Warner and American Youth Football but no large encompassing organization that extends past the youth level. Neither youth organization requires score reporting but both youth leagues have a national championship that brings a few teams from different regions together.

Team Size: High

Football teams usually have large teams. At the professional level the roster is about 50 people. Youth leagues  normally are limited by participation but given there are enough sign ups they average about 30 people per team (11 for both offense and defense, a kicker, a punter, and a few subs).

Team vs. Individual: High

Football is a team sport with lots of opportunity for analysis based on player interaction. Coaches will benefit from insight on what quarterback/receiver teams have the most success together. Even though there are many individual statistics kept about football they all pertain to the same play and the same game.

Culture

Acceptance of Technology: High

At a professional and college level, football teams are employing new technologies like sensors in pads to track players on the field,wearable technology, and tablet playbooks. Even at the high school level some teams use a company that allows them to upload game film to a cloud server and edit and add comments to send back to players.

Appreciation for Statistics: High

With so many unqiue positions, there are a lot of statistics to be kept in football and a lot are used in reporting games. Fans have a desire to see statistics in the media. There are widely used advanced statistics - such as QBR  rating - and also other advanced analytics that are starting to become more widely used.  

Pain Points

Field Position: High

Field position is very important in football because a team needs to gain a certain number of yards on the field in order to ultimately score. Scorekeepers need to record on which yard line each aspect of the play takes place to determine passing yards, running yards, and other statistics.

Game Clock: High

Football requires stopping and starting the clock between every play. There's also a game clock that determines the maximum time between plays before a penalty is given. Clock management is a very important part of football strategy.

Benefit from Automation: High

Scorekeepers have to record a lot of different information for each play - whether it was a pass or run, whether it was compelted, how many yards, who was involved - in addition to recording individual passing and rushing stats. By inputting the roster ahead of time, a scorekeeper can easily select the players involved in the play and then the individual statistics will be automatically compiled.

By using a visual representation of the playing field to record plays it will automate the process of recording yardage.

Additional Insights

Existing Competition: Low

There are many existing football scorekeeping and statistics recording apps. Some include iScore, iTouchStats, and TurboStats to name a few. There are also many other websites and apps that offer similar services.  Some are aimed towards scouts and some offer team management features such as communication with players and live updates. 

Benefits from Live Updates: High

Football's structure allows for meaningful updates of the state of the game after each play. The finite nature of the plays in fooball easily faciliates play-by-play updates. This technology is widely used at professional and college levels and in some other youth sports applicaitons.
