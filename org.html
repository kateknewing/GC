Organizational Structure

Subfactor Breakdown

Governing Body Requirements

Many youth organizations have a national body that serves as a central source for rules and communication between leagues. A strong governing body can benefit GameChanger for many reasons. If the organization requires score reporting, GameChanger can simplify that process for leagues. The community of separate organizations across the nation also allows for early adopters of GameChanger to encourage fellow leagues to adopt.

Low Medium High

No central governing body exists or few teams belong to it and there is little interaction between members.

The governing body collects no data and has no structural requirements in regards to scorekeeping

A central governing body exists and a moderate number of teams belong to it. Members interact with each other.

Publish rules and scoring guidelines for members to follow. May collect scoring information from members.

A central governing body exists and most youth programs belong to it.

Central organization collects scoring information in a central database.
Team Composition

Sports that are made up of unique positions with unique data collection opportunities are beneficial to GameChanger because of the additional opportunity for data collection and analysis. Sports in which all positions have the same statistics don't have as many opportunities for robust analytics.

Low Medium High
Data recorded for each position is the same or very similar Some positions require unique statistics and data collection Completely different statistics and data recorded for many different positions
Team Size

Team size is based on the number of players competing at once combined with the average size of a roster a team normally keeps. A larger team size is beneficial to GameChanger because one coach who adopts the app will influence a larger amount of players and families.

Low Medium High
Smaller team roster. Few kids compete at once. Moderately sized roster. Five to nine people on the field at a time. Large team size. Ten or more players on the field at a time.
Team versus Individual Scoring

Sports where the entire team is scored as a whole are more desirable for GameChanger than sports where each player is scored individually. In individual sports, players on the same team are often competing in different events at the same time, but for team sports a coach can easily collect data on each of his players by keeping track of the game. Team sports also offer opportunities for analysis based on interactions of players.

Low High
Each athlete is scored individually and then the scores are aggregated to determine the team score. The team competes and is scored as one whole entity.
