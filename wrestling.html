Wrestling

Pros
  • Demand for live updates throughout a match.
  • Automation would allow for more stats to be recorded.
Cons
  • Lack of youth 6-12 organizations.
  • No positions that would provide separate stats.
  • Wrestling is scored on individual feats.

Factor Analysis

Market Size and Demographics

Wrestling has the 7th highest participation at the high school level. According to NFHS, 278890 high school athletes wrestled in the 2013-2014 school year. This accounts for 3.62% of total high school sports participation. According to SFIA, wrestling has 1,829,000 participants where 49.9% of participants are between the ages of 6 to 17.

Wrestling has seen a negative 5 year growth rate - down 10.9% and up 0.16% in high school participation.

Organizational Structure

Governing Body Requirements: Medium

There is a nationwide organization NWCA (National Wrestling Coaches Association), which covers high school and college across all 50 states. It provides team schedules, rosters, and statitics for each wrestler. There are many small organizations like AAU and NUWAY which extend into 6-12 youth but are lacking in coverage.

Team Size: Medium

The size of wrestling teams range from 12 - 25 wrestlers. There is only ever one team memeber on the mat at a time for team duals.

Team Composition: Low

Wrestling doesn't have different positions and the same stats are recorded for each wrestler throughout a match. The only thing to take note of is individual weight classes, which usually range between 12-17 classes.

Team vs. Individual: Low

Wrestling is an indivdual sport where players contribute to the final score, but the are the deciding factor for if the team recieves points. There is some oppertunity for analytics, but they would only be useful after the match. However, coaches and wrestlers would want to see the stats for up coming opponents, so they could prepare appropriately.

Culture

Acceptance of Technology: Low

At the high school level big wrestling tourtaments are being streamed via Track Wrestling and at the college/olympic level Flo Wrestling is being used to do the same. Track Wrestling also provides tourtament managing software and services primarily only at the largest and most popular toutaments. There are a few apps that track wrestling mathces, but they are not widely accpeted.

Appreciation for Statistics: Medium

Wrestling stats are recorded and valued more for accomplishment reasons. Wrestlers keep track of wins, takedowns, pins, escapes, and the points they scored. The speed of the match provides for easy stats recording, therefore there hasn't been a huge push to record more stats.

Demand for Content: High

There is plenty going on during a wrestling match to where updates could be needed every 10-20 seconds. Both an individual's stats/scores and team's score can be updated. During tourtaments multiple updates for wrestlers on the same team would be necessary.

Pain Points

Field Position: Low

Positioning in wrestling is of no relevance, because matches happen one on one within a bounded area. As far as field position goes the wrestler has three different starting positions throughout the match.

Game Clock: Medium

Wrestling matches usually have some stopping and starting of the game clock depending on if a wrestler is injuried or out of bounds. There is one score keeper who records times during each of the 3 periods whenever falls(pins), injury, or tech falls(More than 15 points scored) occur.

Pace of Game: Medium

Wrestling has a moderate pace with score keeping that is needed every 10-20 seconds, because the wrestlers are constantly trying to score points against each other within a given period. There are 3 periods between 1-3 minutes depending on age group.

Benefit from Automation: High

Scorekeepers could put the rosters in for a match or tourtament ahead of time. They could also record more dynamic stats about the moves used to score the points for a takedown, escape, near fall, and etc.

Additional Insights

Existing Competition: Medium

There are existing tourtament managing services and software like Track Wresling, but it is used for bigger and more costly toutaments. There is also an app called Takedown which provides score keeping, comprehensive analysis, and engages fans after the match with detailed reports.

Benefits from Live Updates: High

Wrestling allows for easy live updating, since each match is one on one and the match is moderately paced. Live updates would allow for fans to see the updating team score, as well as the current wrestler progression throughouht the match.

Advanced Statistics: Medium

Coaches and wrestlers could potetnially gain from more advanced statistics, especially in regards to which moves are the most effecive. With access to their opponents statistics wrestlers could better prepare for mathces knowing how the match will most likely progress and how their opponent will react.
