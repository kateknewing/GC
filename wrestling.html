Wrestling

Pros
  • Demand for live updates throughout a match.
  • Automation would allow for more stats to be recorded.
Cons
  • Lack of youth 6-12 organizations.
  • No player positions that would provide separate stats.
  • Scored on individual feats.

Factor Analysis

Market Size and Demographics

Wrestling has the 7th highest participation at the high school level. According to NFHS, 278,890 high school athletes wrestled in the 2013-2014 school year. This accounts for 3.62% of total high school sports participation. According to SFIA, wrestling has 1,829,000 participants where 49.9% of participants are between the ages of 6 to 17.

According to Sports & Fitness Industry Association (SFIA) wrestling has seen a negative 5 year growth rate down 10.9% overall and according to National Federation of State High School Associations (NFHS) it is up 0.16% in high school participation.

Organizational Structure

Governing Body Requirements: Low

There is a nationwide organization called NWCA (National Wrestling Coaches Association), which covers high school and college across all 50 states. It provides team schedules, rosters, and statistics for each wrestler. There are many small organizations like AAU and NUWAY which extend into 6-12 youth but are lacking in coverage.

Team Size: Medium

The size of wrestling teams range from 12 - 25 wrestlers. There is only ever one team member on the mat at a time for team duals.

Team Composition: Low

Wrestling doesn't have different positions and the same stats are recorded for each wrestler throughout a match. The only thing to take note of is the individual weight classes, which usually range between 12-17 classes.

Team vs. Individual: Low

Wrestling is an individual sport where players contribute to the final score, but they're the deciding factor for if the team receives points. There is some opportunity for analytics, but they would only be post match. However, coaches and wrestlers would want to see the stats for upcoming opponents, so they could prepare appropriately.

Culture

Acceptance of Technology: Medium

At the high school level big wrestling tournaments are being streamed via Track Wrestling and at the college/Olympic level Flo Wrestling is being used to do the same. Track Wrestling also provides tournament managing software and services primarily only at the largest and most popular tournaments. There are a few apps that track wrestling matches, but they are not widely accepted.

Appreciation for Statistics: Medium

Wrestling stats are recorded and valued more for accomplishment reasons. Wrestlers keep track of wins, takedowns, pins, escapes, and the points they scored. The speed of the match provides for easy stats recording, therefore there hasn't been a huge push to record more stats.

Demand for Content: High

There is plenty going on during a wrestling match to where updates could be needed every 10-20 seconds. Both an individual's stats/scores and team's score can be updated. During tournaments multiple updates for wrestlers on the same team would be necessary.

Pain Points

Field Position: Low

Positioning in wrestling is of no relevance, because matches happen one on one within a bounded area. As far as field position goes the wrestler has three different starting positions throughout the match.

Game Clock: High

Wrestling matches usually have some stopping and starting of the game clock depending on if a wrestler is injured or out of bounds. There is one score keeper who records times during each of the 3 periods, they specifically record times whenever falls (pins), injury, or tech falls (More than 15 points scored) occur.

Pace of Game: Medium

Wrestling has a moderate pace with score keeping that is needed every 10-20 seconds, because the wrestlers are constantly trying to score points against each other within a given period. There are 3 periods between 1-3 minutes depending on age group.

Benefit from Automation: Low

Scorekeepers could put the rosters in for a match or tournament ahead of time. They could also record more dynamic stats about the moves used to score the points for a takedown, escape, near fall, and etc.

Additional Insights

Existing Competition: Medium

There are existing tournament managing services and software like Track Wrestling, but it is used for bigger and more costly tournaments. There is also an app called Takedown which provides score keeping, comprehensive analysis, and engages fans after the match with detailed reports.

Benefits from Live Updates: High

Wrestling allows for easy live updating, since each match is one on one and the match is moderately paced. Live updates would allow for fans to see the updating team score, as well as the wrestlers progression throughout the match.
