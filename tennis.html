Tennis

Pros
  • Fast paced and opportunity for advanced statistics
  • 32% of tennis players have a household income greater than $100k
  • Strong central governing body (USTA)
Cons
  • Only 36% of participants are between the ages 6-17.
  • No positions that would provide separate stats.
  • Tennis is scored on individual feats.

Factor Analysis

Market Size and Demographics

Tennis has the 6th highest participation at the high school level. According to NFHS, 338363 high school athletes wrestled in the 2013-2014 school year. This accounts for 4.39% of total high school sports participation.

Tennis's high school growth rate is down .22%. According to TIA (Tennis Industry Association) The gender split is fairly even with males making up 56.9% and females 43.10%.

Organizational Structure

Governing Body Requirements: High

There is a nationwide organization the USTA (United States Tennis Association), which provides a way for schools to bring tennis programs to their schools. They provide services for all age groups and use a system called TennisLink to connect everyone, manage leagues, and offer a place to upload their stats too.

Team Size: Medium

The size of tennis teams range from 7 - 12 participants. This is all dependent on the age group with juniors having an average of 8 and high/college around 11 players.

Team Composition: Low

Athletes are scored individually and the results of their matches count toward the team score. There are no separate stats recorded for singles or doubles, because there are no distinct positions.

Team vs. Individual: Low

Tennis is an individual sport where players contribute to the final score, they are the deciding factor for if the team receives points. There is some opportunity for analytics, but they would only be useful after the match, since coaches are unable to advise their player during a match. However, coaches and players would want to see the stats for upcoming opponents, so they could prepare appropriately.

Culture

Acceptance of Technology: High

In the professional leagues tennis has Hawk-Eye which tracks the tennis ball and player's positioning on the court. IBM's PointStream technology provides analytic assessments of players and statistics. 3D technology has been used to track a player's movement and determine the risk for injury for each serve.

Appreciation for Statistics: Low

Tennis at the professional level has the ability to access advanced analytics, but at the youth level the game is so fast that only the score of each set is recorded. More advanced stats could be recorded, but paper and pencil only allows minimal scoring.

Demand for Content: Medium

Tennis scores are changing often enough to where live updates would provide a constant stream of information. Live advanced stats would require for more advanced technology or a better scoring systems to be able to keep track of the match, since tennis is one of the fastest scoring sports.

Pain Points

Field Position: Low

Positioning in tennis is of no relevance, because matches happen between 2 - 4 players on one court. Doubles does require positioning, but no distinct stats are taken for the front and back players.

Game Clock: Low

No time is kept in tennis, so a player keeps on playing until a match has been decided.

Pace of Game: High

Tennis has a fast pace with constant scoring and player actions happening on the court. Advanced stats could be recorded every 1-2 seconds, because of the back and forth racketing of the ball.

Benefit from Automation: High

Scorekeepers could keep track of the types of returns and serves. For example if it was a back hand return, which resulted in a fault. Also tracking where the ball had landed would provide insight into a player's accuracy.

Additional Insights

Existing Competition: Medium

The existing competitors include: Track Tennis, Tennis Trakker, and Tennis Link. These apps cover it all, Tennis Link provides leagues and users bases for players to join and upload there stats too. Track Tennis provides a way to score a tennis game via a mobile device and record more in-depth stats. Lastly, Tennis Trakker provides visualization tools for stats recorded on the app.

The market is most of the basis covered with Tennis Link being the most widely accepted with a 330,000 users.

Benefits from Live Updates: High

Live tennis updates would be extremely useful, because the game happens so fast. There is constant action and scoring on the court with the ability to track these events given better tools. This would result in a constant stream of live updates that could be provided to fans.
